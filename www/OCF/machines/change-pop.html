Changing your POP server

Anyone who uses a POP program (Eudora, NuPOP, Netscape, etc.) to read their OCF mail on another computer should change their settings on that program to connect to the POP and/or SMTP servers at the address mail.OCF.Berkeley.EDU instead of ocf.berkeley.edu or whichever of our disaster-named machines you currently use.

How you actually make this change depends on the client you are using. We've assembled the following instructions here - let us know if you have any corrections or additions to this list.

Eudora for Macintosh or Windows
From the Special menu, choose Settings. Select Getting Started from the configuration panels on the left. Go to the field for POP account and change the entry to user@mail.ocf.berkeley.edu (replace user with your account name).

If you have any problems, check out the Getting Started with Eudora for Macintosh or Getting Started with Eudora for Windows pages provided by the UCB Workstation Support Group.

Netscape Navigator 2.0, 2.01, 2.02, or 3.0beta
From the Options menu, choose Mail and News Preferences. Click on the Servers tab at the top of the dialog box. Enter mail.OCF.Berkeley.EDU in both the Outgoing Mail (SMTP) Server and Incoming Mail (POP) Server boxes.

If you have any problems, check out the Netscape Handbook for your version of Navigator in the Help menu.
