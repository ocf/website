in any case, the argument that the university should continue to provide
dial-up internet access because problems with sprintlink often cut off
berkeley.edu from isps seems to make little sense -- money is going to be
spent to fix the "sprinltink problem" one way or another, and until it's
fixed one will have problems whether on-campus or dailing in from outside
berkeley.edu.

as far as restricting the modems to berkeley.edu, the idea is almost
(not in a technical but theoretical sense) to convert the modem banks into
a defacto dail-in bbs for things like the BIK, not to make a misguided
attempt at limiting internet use to "educational purposes." if people are
_only_ using the campus modems for site-licensed/berkeley.edu-restricted
data, the load would be far lower. then, when the campus develops a secure
(proxy-based?) system for accessing restricted stuff from outside
berkeley.edu, the modems can be dissolved completely.
