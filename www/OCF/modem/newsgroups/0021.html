Here's my proposal - maximum of 60 hours per week.  If you go over,
your home-ip account is yanked for a month.  Has no effect on small
users, and could be implimented fairly easily compared to a system
that actually kicked you off after a certain amount of time.  I
remember than the logistics of it was why they didn't want to do a
time limit (per week I mean), but it should be trivial to
automatically shut off the accounts of those who have used a certain
amount of time in the previous week.  Set the number above what anyone
would reasonably use in a week, and you get rid of those who try to
keep their machines online 24/7.  (This is my main complaint - the
home-ip policy clearly states that you are only supposed to use the
account when you are using the computer, but it seems that DCNS
refuses to enforce this policy.)
