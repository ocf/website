ryan travis tate <ryantate@uclink.berkeley.edu> wrote:
>the whining over 2 hour modem time limits and suggestions of rationing or
>charging for campus modem use are silly and ill-conceived. if ucb hadn't,
>as a key part of the internet in its pre-commercial days, offered modem
>access to students and faculty, no one would have the gall to suggest it
>has any duty to do so now.

>no more than it is obliged to pay for students' books, personal computer,
>cable service, backpack, bluebook, notebook paper or any other key


Certainly, the University is not obligated to provide modems
nor textbooks for students as you correctly pointed out.
However, the University does have the funds to provide
certain services that it considers useful to the campus
community - such as a shuttle bus, night escort service,
etc.  To say that the "whining" over the modems is silly
and ill-conceived, just because you don't think the
University should provide that service, is stupid.
